Maya,

I've been thinking about the second-career student you met at the spring event — particularly your point that the path back into nursing from another field is the one the scholarship was, frankly, not designed for. You're right, and the fact that you're still thinking about him three months later is the kind of attention this program needs from the people who fund it.

Your question about whether a second-career carve-out is operationally feasible is the right one to be asking. Honestly, I don't know yet. The mechanics are not complicated; the politics — internal to the school of nursing — are slightly more so. Give me two weeks and I'll come back with a real proposal: what it would take to set up, what it would change, what it would cost in administrative time, and where the awkward conversations would need to happen.

A current-cohort note you'd want to know: the NCLEX first-attempt pass rate on this year's graduating scholars is 94%, against the school's 87% overall. The placement rate at hospitals within thirty miles is 100%; six of seven are at Adventist sites.

Warmly,
Elan

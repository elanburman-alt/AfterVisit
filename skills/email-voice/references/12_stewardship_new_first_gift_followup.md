Tomás,

Thank you for the gift in June, and for lunch on Wednesday. I came away with a clearer sense of why you gave than most first-gift conversations produce.

Your question about how the program tracks outcomes was direct, so the answer should be too. We measure short-term symptom reduction at thirty and ninety days, return-to-functional-baseline self-reports at six months, and re-presentation rates at one year. We do not yet measure what most programs in this space don't measure — the longer arc, two and three years out — and I'm not going to pretend the one-year data tells the whole story. Andrea on the program team has been honest with me about that, and she'd be honest with you too.

The most recent outcomes summary, with those caveats noted, will be in your inbox within ten days. If a quick on-site visit in early fall would help you decide whether the program is doing what we say it's doing, I'll set it up — Andrea is the right person to spend time with. Equally fine to wait until the data convinces you on its own.

Warmly,
Elan
